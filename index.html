Predicting and Designing Novel Materials via Supercomputers

We combine quantum mechanical principles, High-Performance Computing (HPC), and cutting-edge Density Functional Theory (DFT) frameworks to model structural transitions, electronic configurations, and multi-functional parameters of emergent materials.

Research Strands

Energy Storage & Solar Materials

First-principles modeling of thin-film hybrid perovskite tandem architectures, micro-porous MOFs, and solid-state battery pathways to enhance multi-junction electrochemical efficiencies.

Magnetism & Phase Stability

Investigating quaternary/half-Heusler configurations, magneto-caloric matrices, and spin-polarized topologies under intense pressures via high-throughput supercomputing workflows.

Electronic Structure Calculations

Mapping linear and non-linear optical vectors, carrier metrics, and localized excited states across intricate strongly correlated electron profiles.
